Step-by-step

How to get started with Midjourney

1

Get a subscription

Go to midjourney.com and subscribe. Plans start at $10/month for ~200 images. No free tier — but quality is unmatched.

2

Write your first prompt

In the web app, type a description of the image you want. Be specific: subject, style, lighting, mood, and composition all matter.

3

Use parameters

Add parameters at the end: --ar 16:9 (aspect ratio), --v 7 (version), --style raw (photorealistic), --q 2 (quality).

4

Explore variations

Click U1-U4 to upscale or V1-V4 to create variations. Use the 🔄 icon to completely regenerate.

5

Master style references

Use --sref [image URL] to match the style of an existing image. Use --cref [image URL] for character consistency.

FAQ

Frequently asked questions

Does Midjourney have a free trial?▾
Midjourney removed its free trial in 2023. All plans require a paid subscription starting at $10/month. The Basic plan gives approximately 200 images per month.
What's the difference between --style raw and default?▾
--style raw produces more photorealistic, less stylized images. The default style adds Midjourney's characteristic artistic enhancement. For product photography or portraits, --style raw is usually better.
How do I get consistent characters across images?▾
Use the --cref (character reference) parameter with a URL of your character image: 'your prompt --cref https://your-image-url.com --cw 100'. The --cw value (0-100) controls how closely it follows the reference.
Can I use Midjourney images commercially?▾
Pro plan and above subscribers own commercial rights to their generated images. Basic plan subscribers ($10/mo) may use images commercially only if their income is under $1M/year. Always check the latest terms.
