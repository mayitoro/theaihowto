Prompt Engineering: Advanced Techniques That Actually Work

Master the most powerful prompting techniques: chain-of-thought, few-shot prompting, role prompting, and more. Get dramatically better results from any AI model.

📖

Introduction

The difference between a mediocre and exceptional AI response often has nothing to do with which model you're using — it's entirely about how you write the prompt. This guide covers the techniques used by AI professionals to get consistently outstanding results from any model.

The six core techniques

Master these techniques

1

Role prompting

Give the AI a specific identity before your task. Compare: "Write a marketing email" vs "You are a direct-response copywriter with 20 years of SaaS experience. Write a marketing email for..." The second version gets dramatically better results.

2

Chain-of-thought (CoT)

For complex reasoning, ask the AI to think step by step. Adding "Think through this step by step before answering" dramatically improves accuracy on math, logic, and multi-step problems.

3

Few-shot prompting

Show the AI exactly what you want before asking. Two or three examples transform vague instructions into precise results. Example → Output. Example → Output. Now ask for your real task.

4

Constraint specification

Define what you do NOT want as well as what you do. Word limits, format requirements, things to avoid, and tone requirements dramatically improve precision.

5

Output format control

Specify the exact format you need: JSON, bullet points, numbered lists, tables. The AI follows format instructions reliably when they are explicit. Add: "Return ONLY a JSON object with this exact structure, no other text".

6

Iterative refinement

Treat prompting as a conversation, not a single shot. Send a prompt, evaluate the result, then refine. Keep good elements and fix bad ones: "Keep the structure but make the tone warmer. Also add a section on..."

Pro tips: Do this

  • ✓More context is almost always better — AI models don't get bored with detail
  • ✓Specify what success looks like: 'A good response will include X, Y, and Z'
  • ✓Ask the AI to identify ambiguities before answering complex questions
  • ✓For creative tasks, give examples of content you admire as reference

Common mistakes: Avoid these

  • ✗Writing prompts as search queries instead of instructions
  • ✗Not specifying format — vague prompts get vague formats
  • ✗Skipping role context for professional/technical tasks
  • ✗Treating every prompt as a one-shot attempt instead of iterating
