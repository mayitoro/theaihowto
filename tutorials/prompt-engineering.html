Prompt Engineering: Advanced Techniques That Actually Work

Master the most powerful prompting techniques: chain-of-thought, few-shot prompting, role prompting, and more. Get dramatically better results from any AI model.

📖
What you'll learn: Master the most powerful prompting techniques: chain-of-thought, few-shot prompting, role prompting, and more. Get dramatically better results from any AI model.
Introduction

The difference between a mediocre and exceptional AI response often has nothing to do with which model you're using — it's entirely about how you write the prompt. This guide covers the techniques used by AI professionals to get consistently outstanding results.

Step-by-step guide

Follow these steps

1

Role prompting

Give the AI a specific identity before your task. Compare: 'Write a marketing email' vs 'You are a direct-response copywriter with 20 years of experience writing emails for SaaS companies. Write a marketing email for...' The second version gets dramatically better results.

// Basic prompt Write a marketing email about our new feature. // Role-prompted version Act as a senior copywriter who specializes in B2B SaaS. Your emails consistently achieve 40%+ open rates. Write a marketing email announcing our new [feature]. Target audience: CTOs at mid-size companies. Goal: book a demo call. Use social proof and urgency.
2

Chain-of-thought (CoT)

For complex reasoning tasks, ask the AI to think step by step. Adding 'Think through this step by step before answering' or 'Let's work through this systematically' dramatically improves accuracy on math, logic, and multi-step problems.

// Without CoT (often incorrect) A train leaves New York at 8am going 60mph. Another leaves Chicago (800 miles away) at 9am going 80mph. When do they meet? // With CoT (much more accurate) Solve this step by step, showing your work at each stage: A train leaves New York at 8am going 60mph...
3

Few-shot prompting

Show the AI examples of exactly what you want before asking for your real output. Two or three examples transform vague instructions into precise results.

// Few-shot example Convert these sentences to a professional tone: Input: 'We screwed up the launch' Output: 'The launch did not meet our planned objectives' Input: 'The client is being difficult' Output: 'The client has expressed concerns that require careful attention' Input: 'John dropped the ball on this' Output: [AI will now match the exact style and format]
4

Constraint specification

Define exactly what you don't want as well as what you do. Constraints dramatically improve precision: word limits, format requirements, things to avoid, and tone requirements.

5

Output format control

Specify the exact format you need. JSON, bullet points, numbered lists, tables, or a specific document structure. The AI will follow format instructions reliably when they're explicit.

Analyze this customer feedback and return ONLY a JSON object with this exact structure, no other text: { "sentiment": "positive|negative|neutral", "score": 0-10, "key_themes": ["theme1", "theme2"], "action_required": true|false, "priority": "high|medium|low" }
6

Iterative refinement

Treat prompting as a conversation, not a single shot. Send a prompt, evaluate the result, then refine. Keep good elements and fix bad ones: 'Keep the structure but make the tone warmer. Also add a section on...'

Pro tips

Do this

  • ✓More context is almost always better — AI models don't get bored with detail
  • ✓Specify what success looks like: 'A good response will include X, Y, and Z'
  • ✓Ask the AI to identify ambiguities before answering complex questions
  • ✓For creative tasks, give examples of content you admire for reference
Common mistakes

Avoid these

  • ✗Writing prompts as search queries instead of instructions
  • ✗Not specifying format — vague prompts get vague formats
  • ✗Skipping role context for professional/technical tasks
  • ✗Not iterating — treating every prompt as a one-shot attempt
