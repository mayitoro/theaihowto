Automate Your Business with AI — No Coding Required

Connect ChatGPT and Claude to your business tools without writing code. Build automations that save hours every week using Make, Zapier, or n8n.

📖
What you'll learn: Connect ChatGPT and Claude to your business tools without writing code. Build automations that save hours every week using Make, Zapier, or n8n.
Introduction

You don't need to be a developer to build powerful AI automations. Tools like Make, Zapier, and n8n let you connect ChatGPT and Claude to Gmail, Slack, Notion, Airtable, Google Sheets, and hundreds of other apps — all through a visual drag-and-drop interface.

Step-by-step guide

Follow these steps

1

Choose your automation platform

Zapier: Easiest to use, 6,000+ apps, paid from $20/mo. Best for beginners. Make: More powerful, visual flow builder, better for complex automations. Free tier available. n8n: Open-source, self-hostable, unlimited flexibility. Best for developers.

2

Build your first automation: Email summarizer

Connect Gmail → ChatGPT → Slack. Every important email you receive gets automatically summarized and posted to a Slack channel. Prompt: 'Summarize this email in 2 sentences: 1) what the sender wants, 2) what action is needed.'

3

Create an auto-responder

Connect your contact form → ChatGPT → send a personalized reply. ChatGPT drafts a response based on the inquiry topic, which you can review before sending — or auto-send for common queries.

4

Build a content pipeline

Google Alerts → Make → ChatGPT → Google Docs. When a news alert triggers for your industry keywords, ChatGPT summarizes the article and adds it to a Google Doc morning briefing.

5

Automate social media posts

Google Sheets → Make → ChatGPT → Buffer. Maintain a spreadsheet of content topics. The automation drafts posts for each platform (LinkedIn, X, Instagram) in the right format, tone, and length.

6

Connect to the ChatGPT API in Make

In Make, search for OpenAI in the modules list. Connect your API key, select Create a Completion, and map dynamic data from previous modules into your prompt using double curly braces: {{email_body}}.

Pro tips: Do this

  • ✓Start with a simple 2-step automation before building complex flows
  • ✓Always add an error handler so failed steps don't silently break your workflow
  • ✓Use ChatGPT to write your automation prompts — meta-prompting works great
  • ✓Test with real data before activating automations that send emails or messages

Common mistakes: Avoid these

  • ✗Building complex automations before testing simple ones
  • ✗Not handling errors — always add error routes in Make/Zapier
  • ✗Using vague AI prompts in automations — be extremely specific about format
  • ✗Forgetting rate limits — the OpenAI API has limits per minute
