Same prompt tested in 6 different AI tools — surprising results

I ran the same prompt through 6 different AI tools and documented everything. The prompt:

"You are a marketing consultant. 
Write a 3-email welcome sequence for a new subscriber 
to a newsletter about personal finance. 
Each email should be under 200 words."

Results summary:

  • Claude Sonnet 4.5 🥇 — Best overall. Most human-sounding copy, great structure, followed instructions perfectly (all under 200 words). Had the best subject lines by far.
  • ChatGPT-5.5 🥈 — Very good but slightly more generic. Subject lines were clickbait-y. Email 3 was 217 words (exceeded limit).
  • Gemini 3 Pro 🥉 — Solid but too formal. Good for corporate brands, wrong for personal finance newsletters. Did follow the word count.
  • Copilot (GPT-5.5 base) — Similar to ChatGPT but with some weird formatting quirks. Good enough for most uses.
  • Perplexity — Not really designed for this. Output was fine but it kept adding research citations to a creative writing task.
  • Grok 4 — Surprisingly good! Had the most personality of any model. Email 1 was excellent. Emails 2 and 3 dropped off in quality.

Takeaway: Use the right tool for the job. For creative/marketing copy: Claude or Grok. For technical tasks: Claude wins consistently. For research: Perplexity. For daily general use: ChatGPT or Copilot.
