How do I stop Claude from cutting off long responses?

Hi everyone, I've been using Claude heavily for writing long documents and I keep running into the same issue: Claude cuts off its response mid-sentence, especially when I ask it to write something long like a detailed report or a full chapter.

I'm on the free tier. Is this a plan limitation? A context window issue? Is there a prompting trick to avoid it?

For example, I ask: "Write a 2,000-word guide on Python best practices" and it stops at around 800 words every time.

This is one of the most common Claude questions! There are several things you can try:

  • 1. Explicitly ask it to continue: When it stops, just reply "Please continue from where you left off." Claude will pick up exactly where it stopped.
  • 2. Set an output target: Instead of "write 2,000 words", try "Write a detailed guide in 5 sections. Each section should be at least 300 words. Do not stop until all 5 sections are complete."
  • 3. Use "Continue..." at the end of your prompt: Add "Do not truncate this response. If you reach a limit, write [CONTINUED] and I will ask you to continue." at the end of your prompt.
  • 4. Break it into parts: For very long content, ask Claude to first create an outline, then fill in each section one at a time. This is actually the most reliable method.

The free tier does have shorter output limits. Claude Pro ($20/mo) gives you significantly longer responses and fewer interruptions.

Adding to the best answer above — the most reliable trick I've found is the "chunk method":

Write Section 1 of 5 of my Python guide: [topic].
Once done, stop and wait for me to say "next" before writing section 2.

This way you control the pacing and Claude never runs out of steam in the middle of a section. Works 100% of the time for me.

Also worth noting: Claude's output limits are per-response, not per-conversation. The context window (200k tokens) is about how much it can read, not how much it can write in one go.

My workflow for long documents:

  • Ask for an outline first (fast, no truncation)
  • Paste the outline back and ask for section 1
  • When done, paste section 1 + outline and ask for section 2
  • This keeps context clean and responses complete
