Hey community! I've been experimenting with AI tools for content for the past 8 months and finally have a workflow that's cut my production time by 65%. Sharing everything because this community has helped me so much.

My full workflow for a 1,500-word blog post:

  • Step 1 — Research (10 min): Use Perplexity AI to research the topic. It cites sources, so I get real data fast. Prompt: "Research [topic] and give me 5 surprising statistics, key debates, and the 3 most authoritative sources."
  • Step 2 — Outline (5 min): Paste the research into Claude and ask for a detailed SEO-optimized outline. Claude is better at structure than ChatGPT in my experience.
  • Step 3 — First draft (15 min): Give Claude the outline + research and ask for a full draft. I use this prompt from the prompt library: the SEO Blog Post one.
  • Step 4 — Humanize (10 min): I paste into ChatGPT-5.5 and ask it to make it sound more like me. "Rewrite this in a conversational tone, add 2 personal anecdotes I can fill in later, remove all corporate buzzwords."
  • Step 5 — Images (5 min): Use Midjourney for featured images. The product photo prompt from our library works great for tech content.

Total time: ~45 minutes vs 3-4 hours before AI. Happy to answer questions!

Great question! The short answer: no, as long as you edit it. Google cares about quality and originality of ideas, not how you wrote the first draft.

My rule: every article I publish, I add:

  • At least 2 personal stories or original examples
  • My own opinion section
  • Original research or data when possible

The AI gives me a scaffold. I build the house. No penalties in 8 months and actually rankings have improved because I publish more consistently now.
